## Tillie's Story

Tillie - also known as *Sprinkles* - is a compact, upside‑down‑loving green‑cheeked conure of the genus *Pyrrhura*. She and her brother Pudge were adopted together on New Year's Day 2018.

### The Early Days

From the beginning, Tillie showed her spirited nature. Her very first bath was in the drinking water - an economy bathtub and a sign of things to come. She and Pudge quickly became a team: scaling a newly‑made rope from the bottom of the cage to the top, snuggling down together after a long week of getting used to their new home, and smacking each other down in play‑fights where even the referee couldn't stay neutral.

Tillie was second‑in‑command - described as **"2IC"** during the great flock merge when Chip arrived. While Pudge (The Boss) met the newcomer outside, Tillie hid inside the cage to prevent conflict - though it all ended with quite a surprise to both her and Pudge.

### A Bird of Spectacular Contrasts

Tillie is a study in delightful contradictions:

- She takes off in spectacular slow‑mo style, *"shooting for the stars"* - but also plummeted mid‑play and landed on an exposed set of toes, tickling them dangerously.
- She's camera‑shy, ducking and weaving - yet runs around in her famous **Tillie Attack Formation**, attacking the camera at every chance with Chip.
- She's a mover, a shaker, a camera avoider - jumping from perch to perch doing *"the bird thing."*

### The Talker

Tillie has a non‑stop attitude and loves to chat. She trains daily on her signature phrase *"Hello Chip, chirp, chirp"* - repetition seems to work! On quieter days you'll catch a *"Hello Tillie,"* some quacks, and even kisses. She's a natural parrot in every sense.

Her trademark "car alarm" call is legendary - you'll know it once you hear it. She sounds the alert when curtain rods are moved, pink water bottles appear, or an overly loud t‑shirt enters the room.

### Bath Time Enthusiast

Tillie loves bath time - mostly. She jumps into the sink on a white dinner plate filled with water, a lean green washing machine who is straight to the point. She's been known to jump the queue at the waterpark slide, cutting in front of poor Chip. Other times, she fibs about wanting a bath and only commits to half of one. You can't always predict a conure.

### The Loyal Companion

Tillie hates being separated from Chip for long periods - it makes her quite worried and visibly distressed. She's used to his eighty‑times grumpier sleepy behaviour and tolerates it with patience. She enjoys company, especially when Chip is nearby. TV or some background radio keeps her mind off things, but human company is what Tillie craves most.

She arrived with completely clipped wings and was stunned to meet Chip, who could fly - *the look on her face, we'll never forget it!* Since then she's become a good flapper, needing two or three exercise outings a day in their busy bird schedules.

Tillie won't hesitate to give you a peck if you hold her up the wrong way or refuse to let her go - but she'll give an adorable warning squawk first. Don't overstay your welcome or she'll show you what she's really made of!
