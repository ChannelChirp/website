**Forever Home:** 19th March 2019 (together with Tillie)  
**Weight:** 64 grams  

## Chip’s Story

Chip came home in a tissue box - a tiny, messy Baby Borb who needed power naps with his sock friend and warm mush from mommy bird. He was the youngest of the flock, but what he lacked in size he more than made up for in personality.

### The Sultana Heist

Within 24 hours of his cage parole, Chip orchestrated the most sophisticated, elaborate sultana heist in conure history - wry, charismatic, and playing entirely by his own rules. Three rules: don’t hurt anybody, don’t steal from anyone who doesn’t deserve it, and play the game like you’ve got nothing to lose.

### The Flock Merge

When Chip arrived, he was the new guy. Pudge was already The Boss, and Tillie - the second‑in‑command - hid inside the cage to prevent conflict while Pudge met the newcomer outside. It all ended with quite a surprise for the resident siblings. Pudge didn’t always play nice; he was caught stomping on poor Chip in what became a tiny opera of bird tyranny. But eventually the three birds found their rhythm, partying around the cage together until the break of dawn.

### The Clumsy Bather

Chip took what can only be described as the longest, most uncoordinated bath in the history of the conures - watched in disbelief by Tillie and Pudge. So adorable! He loves to bathe, though not every day. He’s a little slow and a little clumsy, but absolutely captivating to watch. He’ll be thoroughly soaked by the end, every time. Tillie once jumped the queue at the waterpark slide, cutting right in front of poor Chip.

### Upside‑Down Bird & The Cockroach

Chip enjoys eating his food while almost completely upside down in the food bowl and loves to do **“the cockroach”** - scaling the walls and ceiling of the cage, usually with Tillie hot on his tail. He taunts Tillie and she gives chase, but he can pull all the moves. He is, after all, the cockroach bird.

When he’s all snuggled up, he tries to feed off your ear or just bury himself in your hair. He once snuck into a birdperson’s hair attempting to build a nest. One thing you quickly learn with conures: wear a hat so they cannot nest in your hair.

### Headbanger & Mischief Maker

Chip headbanged with wild abandon - until he accidentally smacked Tillie right in the head while she was eating capsicum. In fairness, she’d been at that bowl for about an hour. He’s also a destroyer of cameras, chasing the lens at every chance, often alongside Tillie in her famous Attack Formation.

He’s been known to bite the people - “Chippy! Why!” - after a long and tiring day. And when Tillie was caged, Chip found his groove, stomping around on the perch like he was king bird.

### The Crash - and Remarkable Recovery

His scariest moment came in February 2019. Chip clipped a piece of metal furniture mid‑flight and couldn’t land properly. Tillie spooked him further with alert noises, causing him to fly around in a panic. He hit a wall, then went straight for the only exposed glass window. His neck was bent, and his cries were like nothing we’d ever heard. We were worried sick - we feared the worst.

He was placed into a dark box with an old t‑shirt to settle. Yet within only 30 minutes, he made a remarkable recovery. Dazed and zoned out at first, then gradually improving - and by day’s end he was upside down, facing the right way, on the road to healing. Baths were off the menu for a few days, and he had a bit of a bump on the noggin, but he was back to his normal, mischievous self in no time.

### Personality & Quirks

Chip is very quiet‑spoken. He’ll occasionally peep out or proclaim a soft “Step up,” but not nearly as often or as loudly as Tillie. He has a sharper beak and claws, can get stuck on things occasionally, and if he wants to bite, he’ll strike fast - lookout! After his second hatchday he should mellow a little more.

Sometimes appearing somewhat absent‑minded, you could think Chip is running on a slightly different track. He doesn’t seem to mind being separated from Tillie, whereas she frets. Perhaps a change really is as good as a holiday.

He enjoys napping in the mid‑afternoon - unlike Tillie, who never seems to sleep. When he’s sleepy, he turns eighty times grumpier. Tillie doesn’t mind though; she’s used to this sort of behaviour.

Chip is also adventurous - the first into the zone of danger, and also the first one to nod off. He shares a sultana box with Tillie one moment, then fights over its remains the next.

### Farewell

The DNA test on fallen feathers suggested both Chip and Tillie were male, though the lab warned against using un‑plucked samples. Whatever the paperwork says, Chip went to his forever home together with Tillie on 19 March 2019.

The very last goodnight chirpies captured a bittersweet moment: *“From tomorrow onward you will be in the care of another. I hope they take good care of you, you will be greatly missed. I won’t forget either of you.”*

*“If there’s trouble, I probably started it.”*
