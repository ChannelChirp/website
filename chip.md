**Species:** Cinnamon green‑cheeked conure

**Nicknames:** Baby Borb, Chippy  
**Favourite foods:** Corn, almonds, apple, salmon, sultanas  
**Personality:** Adventurous and carefree — the lovable clown of the flock  
**Hatchday:** Late 2017  
**Anniversary:** 3 February 2018  
**Forever Home:** 19 March 2019 (together with Tillie)  
**Weight:** 64 grams  

## Chip's Story

Chip came home in a tissue box — a tiny, messy **Baby Borb** who needed power naps with his sock friend and warm mush from mommy bird. He was the youngest of the flock, but what he lacked in size he made up for in personality.[reference:0][reference:1][reference:2]

### The Sultana Heist & Early Mischief

Within 24 hours of his cage parole, Chip orchestrated the most sophisticated, elaborate sultana heist in conure history — wry, charismatic, and playing by his own rules. Three rules: Don't hurt anybody, don't steal from anyone who doesn't deserve it, and play the game like you've got nothing to lose.[reference:3]

He headbanged with abandon — until he smacked Tillie in the head while she was eating capsicum. In fairness, she'd been at that bowl for about an hour.[reference:4]

### The Clumsy Bather

Chip took what was described as "the longest, most uncoordinated bath in the history of the conures" while Tillie and Pudge looked on. Sooooo cute![reference:5] He loves to bathe — though not every day. He is a little slow and a little clumsy, but adorable to watch. He'll be thoroughly soaked by the end of his bath. Tillie once jumped the queue at the waterpark slide, cutting in right in front of poor Chip.[reference:6]

### The Flock Merge

When Chip arrived, he was "the new guy." Pudge was The Boss, and Tillie (2IC) hid inside the cage to prevent conflict. It all ended with quite a surprise to both Tillie and Pudge.[reference:7] Pudge didn't always play nice — he was spotted stomping on Chip in "Stomp! The Musical!" But eventually the three birds partied around the cage together until the break of dawn.[reference:8][reference:9]

### Upside‑Down & Cockroach Bird

Chip enjoys eating his food while almost completely upside down in the food bowl and loves to do **"the cockroach"** — scaling the walls and ceiling of the cage, usually with Tillie in tow! He taunts Tillie and she gives chase, but he can pull all the moves — he is, after all, the cockroach bird.[reference:10]

When he's all snuggled up, he tries to feed off your ear or just bury himself in your hair. He once snuck into a birdperson's hair attempting to build a nest. One thing you need to be very careful about with conures: wear a hat so they cannot nest in your hair.[reference:11][reference:12]

### The Crash — and Recovery

His scariest moment came in February 2019. Chip clipped a piece of metal furniture mid‑flight, then was unable to land properly. Tillie spooked him further with alert noises, causing him to fly around panicked — he hit a wall, then went straight for the only exposed glass window. His neck was bent, his cries were like nothing we'd ever heard. We were worried sick.[reference:13]

We feared the worst — we may have had to have him put down. I put him into a dark box with an old t‑shirt to let him settle. Yet within only 30 minutes, he made a remarkable recovery.[reference:14] Dazed and zoned out at first, then gradually improving — and by day's end he was upside down, facing the right way, on the road to healing.[reference:15][reference:16] Baths were off the menu for a few days though. He's got a bit of a bump on the noggin.[reference:17]

### Personality & Quirks

Chip is very quiet‑spoken; he will occasionally peep out or proclaim "Step up," but not nearly as often or loudly as Tillie. He has a sharper beak and claws, can get stuck on things occasionally, and if he wants to bite you, he'll strike fast — lookout! After his second hatchday he should mellow out a little more.

Sometimes appearing somewhat absent‑minded, you could think Chip is running on a slightly different track, as he doesn't seem to mind being separated from Tillie. Perhaps a change is as good as a holiday!

He enjoys napping in the mid‑afternoon — unlike Tillie. When sleepy, he turns eighty times grumpier. Tillie doesn't mind though; she is used to this sort of behaviour.[reference:18]

Chip is also adventurous — first into the zone of danger, and also the first one to nod off.[reference:19] He shares a sultana box with Tillie one moment, then fights over its remains the next.[reference:20][reference:21] He has been known to bite the people — "Chippy! Why!" — after a long and tiring day.[reference:22] And when Tillie was caged, Chip found his groove, stomping around on the perch like he was king bird.[reference:23]

### Farewell

The DNA test on fallen feathers suggested both Chip and Tillie were male, though the lab warned against un‑plucked samples. Whatever the paperwork says, Chip went to his forever home with Tillie on 19 March 2019.[reference:24]

The very last goodnight chirpies captured a bittersweet moment: *"From tomorrow onward you will be in the care of another. I hope they take good care of you, you will be greatly missed. I won't forget either of you."*[reference:25]
